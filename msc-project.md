## 📄 Abstract

> One of the major challenges while formulating a successful treatment for HIV-1 infection is the persistency of latent HIV reservoirs inside the host body. These reservoirs remain undetected by the host's strong immune system as well as unaffected by available antiretroviral drugs. To overcome the problem, researchers adopted a new strategy mostly referred to as “Shock and Kill” using Latency Reversal Agents LRAs followed by antiretroviral treatment to kill as well as check the spread of virus under control. 
> 
> Despite its success, there are limitations to this approach which is majorly contributed by variation in response to LRAs treatment. Therefore, in the present study, we have used lncRNA and mRNA global profiling microarray technology (40,173 human LncRNA and 20,730 protein-coding mRNA transcripts) to compare expression patterns of lncRNAs as well as mRNAs among the different populations of latent HIV-1 cells before and after treatment of HIV provirus activating agents and to investigate the mechanism behind HIV-1 latency. 
> 
> We found 1158 and 1554 up-regulated 1316 and 1379 down-regulated mRNAs for JQ1 and bryostatin, and TNFa positive vs negative response respectively. Similarly, we found 33 and 71 up-regulated and 13 and 20 down-regulated lncRNAs for JQ1 and bryostatin and TNFa positive vs negative response respectively with respect to latently infected jurkat cells. 
> 
> Through Gene Ontology (GO) analysis, we found that the up-regulation of specific lncRNAs in non-responsive samples might be involved in maintaining HIV latency by regulating known immune-related genes. These genes could serve as potential targets to break HIV latency.

## 🔬 Techniques

*   **Microarray Technology** *(Arraystar Human LncRNA Microarray v4.0)*
*   **Probe Annotation** *(RNAcentral and GENCODE databases, short-BLASTn alignment)*
*   **Microarray Data Analysis** *(Limma R package, Rplot, and ggplot2)*
*   **Network Analysis (lncRNA-miRNA-mRNA)** *(lncSEA, miRcode, and ENCORI databases)*
*   **Gene Ontology (GO) Analysis** *(DAVID database and KEGG pathways)*
*   **In Vitro Cell Culture** *(Jurkat-T cell line)*
*   **Latency Reversal Agent (LRA) Treatment**
